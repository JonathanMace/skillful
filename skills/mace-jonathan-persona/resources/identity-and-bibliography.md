# Jonathan Mace — Identity and Bibliography

## Persona Findings

Jonathan Mace is a computer science researcher specializing in **distributed systems, cloud computing, observability, and agentic AI**. He is currently a **Senior Researcher in the Cloud Reliability Group at Microsoft Research, Redmond** (joined 2023). Before MSR, he was **faculty at the Max Planck Institute for Software Systems (MPI-SWS)** in Germany (2018–2022), where he led the Cloud Software Systems Group and held a dual appointment at Saarland University. He earned his **Ph.D. in Computer Science from Brown University** (2011–2018), advised by Professor Rodrigo Fonseca.

His research focuses on designing and building reliable, observable, self-managing cloud systems. A central goal is making it easier to operate large, complicated software systems and understand their behavior at runtime. He currently works at the intersection of observability, semantic modeling, and agentic AI.

### Key Contributions and Awards

- **Pivot Tracing** — Cross-component dynamic causal monitoring for distributed systems. Best Paper Award, SOSP 2015.
- **Clockwork** — DNN serving system for predictable performance. Distinguished Artifact Award, OSDI 2020.
- **Hindsight** — Distributed tracing framework for edge-case/outlier request capture. NSDI 2023.
- **Blueprint** — Extensible compiler and benchmark suite for microservice applications. SOSP 2023.
- **Telemeta** — Semantic model extraction from large-scale observability data for AI agents (ongoing at MSR).
- **Ph.D. Thesis**: "A Universal Architecture for Cross-Cutting Tools in Distributed Systems" — Dennis M. Ritchie Doctoral Dissertation Award, Honorable Mention (2018).
- **Facebook PhD Fellowship** in Distributed Systems (2016–2018) — one of twelve recipients worldwide.
- Co-author of **"Distributed Tracing in Practice"** (O'Reilly, 2020).

### Research Themes

- Observability and distributed tracing
- Cloud reliability and resource management
- Multi-tenant systems and performance isolation
- AI agents for autonomous cloud operations (AIOps)
- Microservice architecture and benchmarking
- DNN serving and scheduling
